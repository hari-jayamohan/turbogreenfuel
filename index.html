Frequently Asked Questions

What is DEF (Diesel Exhaust Fluid)?

+

DEF is a high-purity solution of 32.5% urea and 67.5% deionized water. It is used in Selective Catalytic Reduction (SCR) systems on diesel engines to reduce harmful nitrogen oxide (NOx) emissions. When injected into the exhaust stream, DEF breaks down NOx into harmless nitrogen and water vapor, helping your fleet meet EPA emissions standards.

How much DEF will my fleet use?

+

DEF is typically consumed at a rate of approximately 2-3% of diesel fuel consumption. For example, if your vehicle uses 100 gallons of diesel, it will consume about 2-3 gallons of DEF. The exact consumption rate depends on engine load, operating conditions, and the specific SCR system design.

What happens if my vehicle runs out of DEF?

+

Modern diesel vehicles are equipped with a DEF gauge that alerts drivers when levels are low. If DEF runs completely out, the vehicle's engine power will be reduced to encourage immediate refilling. This is a built-in feature to ensure emissions compliance. Once the DEF tank is refilled, normal power levels are restored automatically.

Does DEF freeze? Will that damage my system?

+

DEF begins to freeze at 12¬∞F (-11¬∞C). However, freezing does not harm the quality of the DEF or damage your system. DEF expands by approximately 7%, and tanks are designed to accommodate this expansion. During vehicle operation, SCR systems provide heating for the DEF tank and supply lines. If DEF freezes when the vehicle is shut down, the heating system will quickly return it to liquid form, and normal vehicle operation will not be affected.

How should DEF be stored?

+

DEF should be stored in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight and heat sources. Ideal storage temperature is between 12¬∞F and 86¬∞F. DEF has a shelf life of approximately one year when stored properly. It should be kept in sealed containers to prevent contamination. DEF is non-hazardous and non-flammable, making it safe to store on-site.

Is DEF an additive that goes into the diesel fuel tank?

+

No, DEF is not a fuel additive. It is stored in a separate dedicated tank on the vehicle and is never mixed with diesel fuel. DEF is injected directly into the exhaust stream where it reacts with NOx emissions in the SCR system. Putting DEF in the fuel tank or diesel in the DEF tank can cause serious damage to your vehicle.

Does DEF meet quality standards?

+

Yes, all DEF must meet the ISO 22241 international quality standard. This ensures the purity and consistency required for proper SCR system operation. Turbo Green Fuel supplies only premium-grade DEF that meets or exceeds these standards, ensuring optimal performance and protecting your equipment investment.

What materials are compatible with DEF?

+

DEF is corrosive to certain metals including copper, brass, aluminum, and carbon steel. Only approved materials as listed in ISO 22241 standards should be used in contact with DEF. This includes stainless steel, specific plastics, and approved rubbers. Our dispensing equipment and storage solutions are designed with DEF-compatible materials to ensure safe, contamination-free delivery.
